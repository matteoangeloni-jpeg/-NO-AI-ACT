Press kit

Ready-made materials for journalists, school publications, educational newsletters and institutional pages that want to present NO AI ACT accurately. All texts on this page are reusable under CC BY 4.0. Current version: v1.1.0.

01Short description (~40 words)

NO AI ACT is a free, open-source serious game about the EU AI Act: in the browser, with no account, players inspect AI systems in a fictional unregulated city, examine evidence and classify risk. Available in Italian and English.

02Medium description (~100 words)

NO AI ACT is a free, open-source investigative serious game about the European AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689). The player is an inspector in a fictional 2032 city where the AI Act never entered into force: they open case files on 13 AI systems — from social scoring to deepfakes, predictive healthcare to generative models — examine evidence, classify risk, assign responsibility and watch the consequences ripple through the city. A local teacher mode supports classroom use. It runs in the browser with no account and no personal-data collection, in Italian and English. Code is MIT-licensed; content is CC BY 4.0.

03Extended description

NO AI ACT starts from a question: how do you teach a complex regulation like the AI Act to people who are not lawyers? The project's answer is to flip the perspective — show a society where those rules do not exist. In the game's fictional city, AI systems operate unconstrained: social scores deciding access to services, unsupervised hiring algorithms, unlabelled deepfakes, opaque medical triage.

Playing as a civic inspector, you reconstruct each case from the evidence: documents, testimony, technical logs. You decide the risk classification under the AI Act's categories, identify who is responsible (provider or deployer), choose proportionate corrective measures and file a report. A debrief explains why each decision holds or fails, and the city's indicators — trust, rights, innovation — respond over time. The game deliberately simplifies the law for educational purposes and treats ambiguous scenarios as contestable: it is not legal advice, and its educational effectiveness has not yet been empirically validated. Dedicated guides, activities and lesson plans for teachers and trainers are available on this site.

06Suggested editorial angles

  • Teaching law through play: what happens when the AI Act is learned by inspecting an unregulated city rather than reading a textbook.
  • Digital civic education: a free, data-collection-free tool for discussing AI in the classroom.
  • Open source and education: a fully open individual project whose privacy promises are enforced by automated tests.
  • Regulation in reverse: telling the story of the AI Act through its absence — the counterfactual world of 2032.

08Privacy statement (reusable)

NO AI ACT requires no registration and collects no personal data: gameplay decisions never leave the browser and progress is saved locally only. The site's public pages use exclusively aggregate, cookie-free statistics.

How We Ensure Accuracy

Every piece of content on NO AI ACT is grounded in official sources and reviewed for accuracy. This page explains our research methodology and fact-checking process.

Our Research Foundation

All content is based on Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 — the official European AI Act. We review content against official guidance from the European Data Protection Board (EDPB), EU AI Office, and academic research on AI governance. Nothing is invented; everything is grounded in documented sources.

Content Development Process

  1. Source identification: We start with the official regulation text and official guidance documents from EU institutions.
  2. Simplification: Complex legal language is translated into plain language for secondary-school and university audiences. Accuracy is never sacrificed for simplicity.
  3. Expert review: Content is reviewed by people with expertise in AI policy, law, and education before publication.
  4. Game scenario design: Case studies are created based on real-world scenarios and regulatory examples, tested for pedagogical clarity.
  5. Testing: Content is tested with educators and students to ensure clarity, engagement, and factual correctness.

Official Sources We Cite

  • Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 — The official AI Act text (EUR-Lex)
  • European Data Protection Board (EDPB) — AI and GDPR guidance
  • EU AI Office — Implementation guidance and policy updates
  • European Parliament — Legislative history and debate

We do not use secondary sources or summaries; all claims are verifiable against primary legal text.

Fact-Checking Process

All factual claims undergo verification:

  • Article citations: Every reference to a specific article number is verified against the regulation text.
  • Risk categories: Definitions of prohibited, high-risk, limited-risk, and minimal-risk AI are taken directly from regulatory text.
  • Compliance deadlines: Implementation timelines are verified against official timeline announcements.
  • Geographic scope: The regulation applies to AI systems deployed in the EU; this scope is clearly stated on every relevant page.
  • Exemptions and exceptions: When the regulation excludes certain systems (e.g., military AI), this is explicitly noted.

Handling Errors and Updates

If you find an error or have a correction:

  • Report it at GitHub Issues
  • Include the specific page, claim, and the correct information with a source
  • Errors are corrected, and a version note is added to the Changelog

Content accuracy is a core responsibility. We take corrections seriously and appreciate community feedback.

What We Don't Do

  • We don't provide legal advice. NO AI ACT is educational, not legal counsel. Always consult qualified lawyers for compliance decisions.
  • We don't simplify beyond accuracy. If something is ambiguous in the regulation, we say so rather than guessing.
  • We don't speculate about future changes. We describe the regulation as it stands now, not predictions about amendments.
  • We don't hide limitations. If a case study is hypothetical, we label it clearly. If interpretation varies, we mention it.

Quality Assurance Standards

Every page on NO AI ACT meets these standards:

  • Claims are verifiable against official sources
  • Language is clear and accessible (target: secondary-school level)
  • Examples are realistic or explicitly labeled hypothetical
  • Limitations and scope are clearly stated
  • Links to official sources are current and functional
  • Content is reviewed by someone other than the author

Transparency About This Project

  • Open-source: Code is MIT licensed; content is CC-BY 4.0

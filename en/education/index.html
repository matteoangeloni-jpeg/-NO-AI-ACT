02Who it is for

  • Teachers and trainers — secondary school, university and professional training, with materials that work without legal prerequisites.
  • Students and self-learners — a structured path from "what is the AI Act?" to discussing real grey cases.
  • Researchers and AI-literacy communities — a documented, privacy-conscious serious game and its supporting materials, open source and reusable (MIT / CC BY 4.0).

03Why AI Act literacy matters

Automated systems already influence hiring, credit, education and public services. The EU AI Act is the reference framework that says which uses are unacceptable, which need strict safeguards, and when people must be told they are dealing with AI.

Knowing its basic logic is not a lawyer's niche skill: it is part of AI literacy and digital citizenship — the ability to ask of any system: what data does it use, who does it affect, who answers for it, how can a decision be challenged?

04Where the serious game fits

Reading about rules is a start; applying them is where understanding happens. In the NO AI ACT serious game you inspect 11 fictional AI systems, weigh the evidence and file a risk classification — then see the consequences.

A typical learning journey: read the plain-language guide, play one or two cases, then use the activities or lesson plans to turn the experience into discussion. The methodology is explained in serious games for AI regulation.

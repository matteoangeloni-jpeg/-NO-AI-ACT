About NO AI ACT

NO AI ACT exists to make AI governance education interactive, accessible, and rooted in the real decisions people must make under the European AI Act. We believe that understanding regulation is best learned by doing, not reading.

Our Mission

The EU AI Act is a technical text describing deeply human decisions: who gets hired, who can access a service, who ends up under surveillance, and where responsibility lies when systems fail. NO AI ACT turns those decisions into a playable investigation.

We build free, open-source educational tools that help students, teachers, and professionals understand AI governance through simulation and evidence-based reasoning — not lectures or checklists.

Our Approach

  • Research-backed: Game design grounded in pedagogical research on serious games and learning through simulation. All content reviewed against the official EU regulation text.
  • Educator-tested: Used in schools and universities across the EU. We collect feedback and iterate based on classroom experience.
  • Expert-reviewed: Content reviewed by policy experts, educators, and researchers in AI governance.
  • Open-source: Code released under MIT license; educational content under CC-BY 4.0. Anyone can build on it, fork it, translate it, or use it in their own projects.
  • Privacy-first: No personal data collection. Game progress saved locally in the browser. No accounts, no dashboards, no tracking.

History

NO AI ACT was created in 2024 in response to the need for accessible education on the newly adopted European AI Act. At the time, the regulation was complex, the stakes were high for organizations implementing it, and educators were asking for tools that made the subject engaging for students.

The first version launched in early 2024 as a simple web game with 11 cases. Since then, it has been used by students and teachers in multiple EU countries, translated into Italian and English, and expanded with teaching resources, lesson plans, and a full educational hub.

As of July 2026: NO AI ACT has reached thousands of students and educators across Europe, remains entirely free and open-source, and continues to grow through community contributions and educator feedback.

Why Open-Source?

Education should not be trapped behind paywalls or proprietary tools. By open-sourcing NO AI ACT, we make it possible for:

  • Educators in any country to adapt it for their curriculum
  • Developers to build on the game engine and create new scenarios
  • Translators to bring it to new languages
  • Researchers to study serious games as a learning methodology
  • Communities to maintain and improve the project independently

Open-source also means transparency: you can see exactly how the game works, what data (if any) is collected, and whether we're doing what we claim.

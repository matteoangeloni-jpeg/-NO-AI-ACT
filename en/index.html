01Why NO AI ACT exists

The EU AI Act is the regulation that defines when an AI system is prohibited, high-risk, or subject to transparency obligations. It's a technical text, written for experts â€” but the decisions it describes affect real people: who gets hired, who can access a service, who ends up under surveillance.

NO AI ACT is an educational simulation that puts the player in the position of someone who has to assess these systems: gathering evidence, classifying risk, justifying a decision. It doesn't replace the regulation's text, and it won't turn anyone into an expert in an hour â€” it's designed to make a mechanism that stays abstract on paper feel concrete.

02What it is

NO AI ACT puts the player in the role of an inspector in a city where artificial intelligence makes decisions about real people. Each case is a file: read the documents, open the evidence, cite the proof and sign a risk classification following the logic of the European AI Act. It is not a multiple-choice quiz: the right decision, poorly justified, remains contestable.

Investigative

Evidence, not slogans

Every case has documents with a source label. To decide you must cite at least two of them: the reasoning matters as much as the conclusion.

True to the AI Act

Four outcomes

Compliant, partial, contestable, non-compliant. Tell apart prohibited practices, high risk, transparency obligations and legitimate uses.

No friction

In the browser, free

No account, no download, no backend. Open the link and play. Your progress stays local on your device.

03What you learn

NO AI ACT trains you to reason about risk, not to memorise articles. By the end of a session you can tell what the law prohibits from what it asks you to govern.

  • Recognise prohibited AI practices (such as social scoring) and why they are banned.
  • Identify high-risk systems and the obligations they entail.
  • Assess transparency, effective human oversight and deployer responsibility.
  • Understand why a disclaimer or a formal human check is often not enough.
  • Distinguish the context of use: the same system can be legitimate or critical depending on how it is deployed.
  • Argue a decision with evidence: contestability as a measure of quality.

To study beyond the game: the plain-language AI Act guide, the risk categories and the glossary.

04A serious game, not a quiz

The reasoning matters as much as the answer. A quiz has one right answer to remember. NO AI ACT has a case to understand: an AI system, a dossier of evidence, a context that changes what each clue means. The game is designed to help players reason through a decision â€” not just check what they remember. Before classifying a case, players can review the context, weigh the evidence, and consult the rule linked to the case. At the end, a debrief explains why a decision holds up or doesn't â€” not just whether it was "right" or "wrong."

  • Every case's context stays available throughout, not just as a one-time intro text.
  • The rule linked to each case is available during the decision, as support for reasoning â€” not as a ready-made answer.
  • A final debrief helps players understand why a choice wasn't fully correct, not just that it was wrong.

07How to use it

  • Solo: open the investigation and work through the cases at your own pace. Choose language, difficulty and mission.
  • In class: turn on teacher mode for the local end-of-session debrief and export an anonymous summary (.txt / .json).
  • In groups: use a case as a discussion prompt; the "decisive evidence" line makes explicit why a classification holds or is contestable.

09Privacy by design

No personal data leaves your device.

This website uses aggregate, privacy-friendly statistics through Cloudflare Web Analytics to understand how public pages are used and improve the project. The game does not require accounts, does not collect personal data and does not send results, answers or inspection reports to external servers. The playtest form is external, voluntary and separate.

Progress and settings are saved only in the browser's localStorage. The teacher debrief export is local and anonymous.

10Frequently asked questions

What is NO AI ACT?

It is a free investigative serious game about the European AI Act. You investigate artificial intelligence systems, examine the evidence and file a report classifying their risk.

Is NO AI ACT legal advice?

No. It is a simplified educational simulation. It is not legal advice and does not replace the text of the regulation or the opinion of a professional.

Do I need to register or create an account?

No. There is no registration, no account, no backend. The game runs entirely in the browser and progress is stored only locally.

How many cases can I play?

Eleven cases, from the clearest to the most ambiguous: from prohibited social scoring to high-risk systems in healthcare, schools and public administration, up to generative models used downstream.

Is it suitable for classroom teaching?

Yes. It is designed for upper-secondary schools, universities and professional training. There is a teacher mode with a local debrief and missions of varying length. No student data is collected.

Does NO AI ACT collect personal data?

No. The game collects no personal data and does not send results, answers or reports to any server. Public pages use aggregate, privacy-friendly Cloudflare Web Analytics; there are no accounts or classroom dashboards.

How much does it cost?

It is completely free and open source. The code is released under the MIT licence and the educational content under CC BY 4.0.

Is it an official legal source on the AI Act?

No. NO AI ACT is a simplified educational simulation. It does not replace the regulation's text or the advice of a qualified professional.

Can I use it with students or PhD candidates?

Yes. It's designed for classroom use, seminars, or individual study, at university level and within PhD programmes focused on AI governance.

How long does a session take?

A single case takes about 10-15 minutes; a session covering several cases can run 30-45 minutes, depending on available time.

Is it suitable for educators and trainers?

Yes. The game includes a teacher mode with a local debrief, designed to support classroom use without requiring accounts or student data.

Can I propose a teaching or research collaboration?

Yes. The project is open to collaborations with educators, institutions and researchers interested in AI Act training or in serious games as an educational tool.

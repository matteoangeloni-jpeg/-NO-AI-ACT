Citizenship · Rights · Accountability

Digital citizenship and AI regulation

Citizenship has always meant knowing your rights and holding institutions accountable. When institutions decide through automated systems, those skills need an update — that update is the civic side of AI literacy.

Practise as an inspector AI literacy

01Regulation as civic infrastructure

The AI Act is not only a market rulebook; it encodes civic guarantees: some practices are off-limits, high-stakes systems must be contestable and overseen, and people must be told when they interact with machines. Reading it as citizenship — who is protected, from what, with which remedies — turns a technical text into civics material.

02Rights and responsibilities, both ways

  • Citizens: the right to know, to contest, to not be socially scored; the responsibility to exercise judgment rather than outsource it.
  • Institutions: the responsibility to deploy systems they understand, document and genuinely oversee — the game's recurring theme of the deployer who "follows the score".

03The questions a critical student can ask

  • Is an automated system involved in this decision at all? How would I know?
  • What data feeds it, and is that data about the right thing?
  • Who is accountable if it is wrong — and can I reach them?
  • Can the decision be explained and challenged?
  • Would this use be acceptable in a different context? Why here?

These map directly onto the game's inspection loop — evidence, classification, responsibility, contestability — which is what makes it usable as civics practice, not just tech education.

04In the classroom

The citizenship angle works best as debate: the activities include a rights-mapping exercise, and the transparency lesson plan stages a structured discussion on the right to know. Background concepts are in the plain-language guide and glossary.

Educational simulation — not legal advice

NO AI ACT is an independent, open-source educational tool. It deliberately simplifies Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (AI Act) and does not replace the legal text or professional advice.

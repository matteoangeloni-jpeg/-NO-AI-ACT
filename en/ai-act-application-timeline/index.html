The AI Act application timeline

"Is the AI Act already in force?" It is the most common classroom question — and the answer is: yes, but its rules apply in stages. Here are the dates that matter, what already applies and what comes next, in plain language and with official sources.

01The stages at a glance

The AI Act does not apply all at once: it entered into force on 1 August 2024 and its rules become applicable in stages (Art. 113). The key dates:

Staged application of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689
DateWhat becomes applicable
1 August 2024Entry into force of the regulation (not yet the application of its rules).
2 February 2025General provisions, prohibited practices and the AI-literacy obligation (Art. 4).
2 August 2025Rules on GPAI models, governance, notifying authorities and penalties.
2 August 2026General application: most obligations, including Annex III high-risk systems and the transparency duties.
2 August 2027High-risk systems embedded in regulated products (Annex I) and the compliance deadline for GPAI models placed on the market before August 2025.

02Entry into force ≠ application

These are two different moments: entry into force (1 August 2024) makes the regulation part of EU law; application is when obligations actually bind those who build or deploy AI systems. The staged calendar gives time to adapt — the most urgent rules first (the prohibitions), then the bulk of the obligations.

03What already applies (as of the review date)

As of this page's last review (July 2026), the bans on unacceptable practices, the AI-literacy obligation and the GPAI rules apply; general application starts on 2 August 2026. The dates are fixed by the regulation itself; practical implementation (national authorities, guidance, standards) is still evolving — check the official sources below for the current state.

04Where the game comes in

NO AI ACT flips this calendar on its head: its 2032 city is a world where none of these dates ever arrived. Playing a case — for instance the scoring city — makes concrete what the 2025 bans and the 2026 obligations are trying to prevent.

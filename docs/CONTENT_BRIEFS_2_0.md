# Content briefs — organic clusters IT/EN (2.0 sprint)

Four pairs chosen from the eight mission candidates — fewer, stronger pages
(mission §19). Rejected for this release: "AI literacy obligations" (overlaps
alfabetizzazione-ai/ai-literacy → cannibalization risk), "High-risk AI in
education" (overlaps sistemi-ai-ad-alto-rischio + case pages), "GPAI and
downstream responsibility" (overlaps ai-generativa-e-gpai), "Teaching AI
regulation through serious games" (overlaps serious-game-regolazione-ai).

## 1. AI Act application timeline
- Routes: /tempi-applicazione-ai-act/ ⇄ /en/ai-act-application-timeline/
- User intent: informational — "when does the AI Act apply?", dates per obligation.
- Audience: teachers, students, professionals orienting themselves.
- Primary query (IT): "quando entra in vigore ai act" / "ai act date applicazione".
- Primary query (EN): "ai act timeline" / "when does the ai act apply".
- Secondary: entry into force vs application, prohibitions date, GPAI date,
  high-risk transitions, national authorities.
- Scope: staged application of Reg. (EU) 2024/1689 in plain language; what
  is already applicable at the time of writing vs upcoming.
- Exclusions: no legal advice, no compliance checklists, no speculation on
  amendments; clearly separate law vs interpretation.
- Official sources: EUR-Lex (Art. 113 entry into force/application), European
  Commission AI Act pages, European AI Office.
- Related pages: guida-ai-act, categorie-rischio, pratiche-vietate, GPAI.
- Internal links: those 4 + glossary + play CTA + lesson plan.
- Learning value: anchors the game's fictional 2032 counterfactual to the real
  staged rollout.
- Cannibalization: low — no existing page covers dates.

## 2. Deepfakes and transparency
- Routes: /deepfake-e-trasparenza/ ⇄ /en/deepfakes-and-transparency/
- User intent: informational — "are deepfakes illegal in the EU / must AI
  content be labelled?"
- Audience: teachers (media literacy), students, general public.
- Primary query (IT): "deepfake ai act" / "obbligo etichetta contenuti ai".
- Primary query (EN): "deepfake eu law" / "ai act deepfake labelling".
- Secondary: Art. 50 transparency, synthetic content marking, exemptions
  (art/satire caveats as interpretation), platform vs creator duties.
- Scope: what the AI Act requires for deepfakes and synthetic content; how the
  game's case "La città sintetica" dramatizes it.
- Exclusions: defamation/copyright law, national criminal law, tool reviews.
- Official sources: EUR-Lex Art. 50, Commission pages.
- Related pages: obblighi-trasparenza, ai-generativa-e-gpai, case 3.
- Learning value: direct bridge from a playable case to the legal rule.
- Cannibalization: medium vs obblighi-trasparenza → this page is scenario-first
  (deepfakes), that page is duty-first; cross-link explicitly.

## 3. AI in recruitment and employment
- Routes: /ai-nel-lavoro-e-selezione/ ⇄ /en/ai-in-recruitment-and-employment/
- User intent: informational — "is AI-based CV screening allowed?", worker
  monitoring rules.
- Audience: teachers (economics/civics), HR-adjacent professionals, students.
- Primary query (IT): "ai act selezione personale" / "algoritmi assunzioni regole".
- Primary query (EN): "ai act recruitment" / "ai hiring rules eu".
- Secondary: Annex III employment area, emotion recognition at work
  (prohibited), human oversight, deployer duties, contestability.
- Scope: employment-related AI under the AI Act; game cases 2 (colloquio) and
  4 (classe osservata, for minors-vs-workers contrast).
- Exclusions: national labour law, GDPR depth (mention only), vendor advice.
- Official sources: EUR-Lex (Art. 5(1)(f), Annex III(4)), Commission.
- Related pages: sistemi-ai-ad-alto-rischio, pratiche-vietate, per-docenti.
- Learning value: the most career-relevant scenario for students.
- Cannibalization: low — no existing employment-focused page.

## 4. Classroom laboratory guide (complete)
- Routes: /laboratorio-ai-act-in-classe/ ⇄ /en/ai-act-classroom-lab/
- User intent: transactional/instructional — "ready-to-run AI Act lab".
- Audience: teachers and trainers; the strongest linkable asset for education
  outreach (newsletters, OER listings link guides like this).
- Primary query (IT): "laboratorio educazione civica intelligenza artificiale".
- Primary query (EN): "ai act classroom activity lab guide".
- Secondary: 30/60/90-minute paths, printable worksheet + rubric, debrief.
- Scope: a complete lab plan combining the game, the printable worksheet, the
  discussion rubric and existing activities into one runnable sequence.
- Exclusions: no new pedagogy claims, no learning-outcome promises
  (effectiveness not validated — say so).
- Official sources: EUR-Lex for the legal anchors; internal resources for flow.
- Related pages: attivita-didattiche, lezione-introduzione, per-docenti,
  ai-act-per-docenti, teacher quick-start (repo).
- Learning value: converts scattered assets into one teacher journey.
- Cannibalization: medium vs lezione-introduzione (60–90' lesson) → the lab
  guide is the umbrella (3 durations + materials + game integration) and links
  the lesson as one of its blocks.

## Shared quality rules (all four)
People-first prose, intent answered in the lead; law vs transitional dates vs
interpretation explicitly separated; examples never framed as legal advice;
no unsupported statistics; no mass-generated FAQ; full IT/EN editorial parity
(natural EN, not literal translation); §9.6 credibility block on every page
(project responsibility, first published, last reviewed, official sources,
disclaimer, related cases, related teacher resources).

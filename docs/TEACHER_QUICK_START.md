# Teacher Quick Start — NO AI ACT

A short guide to running NO AI ACT in a classroom or training session. No setup,
no accounts, no student data. The game (v1.0.0) has **13 cases**, from prohibited
practices to high-risk and "to be governed" systems.

- Play: <https://www.no-ai-act.eu/play/>
- Overview (IT): <https://www.no-ai-act.eu/>
- Overview (EN): <https://www.no-ai-act.eu/en/>

## In one minute

1. Open the play link in a desktop or laptop browser (tablet in landscape also
   works; smartphones in portrait show a "rotate device" guard).
2. Choose the language (IT / EN), a difficulty and a mission.
3. Turn on **Teacher mode** for a local end-of-session debrief.
4. Play one or more cases; discuss the inspection reports together.
5. Export the debrief (`.txt` / `.json`) if you want a record — it is local and
   anonymous.

## Suggested session lengths

- **Single case (10–15 min):** one file, then a group discussion of the report.
- **Short lab (~30 min):** the "short lab" mission, 2–3 cases.
- **Full session (~60 min):** the full mission across the core cases.
- **Advanced (~75–90 min):** the "advanced cases" mission (chatbot, procurement,
  EdTech, GPAI).

## How to facilitate

- Ask students to **cite the evidence** before deciding, and to justify the
  classification out loud.
- Use the **"decisive evidence"** line to show why a classification holds or is
  *contestable* — a correct call with weak reasoning is not "compliant".
- Compare difficulty levels: *base* forgives minor reasoning gaps and offers a
  hint after a mistake; *expert* is stricter.
- Lean on the norm cards and their "this does not mean that…" cautions to avoid
  over-generalising (e.g. not every chatbot or generative model is prohibited).

## Discussion prompts

- Where is the boundary between **prohibited social scoring** and legitimate
  welfare or credit assessment? (Cases: *The city of scores*, *Civic credit*.)
- When is **human oversight** real rather than a rubber stamp? (Cases: *Invisible
  triage*, *The profiled classroom*.)
- Who is **responsible** — provider, deployer, public authority? (Cases: *The
  opaque tender*, *The do-it-all model*.)
- Does a **disclaimer** discharge transparency duties? (Cases: *The synthetic
  city*, *The desk that always answers*.)

## Privacy for the classroom

- No accounts, no logins, no classroom dashboard.
- No student data is collected or sent anywhere.
- Progress is stored only in the browser on the device used.
- The teacher export is local and anonymous; share it at your discretion.

## Good to know

- The game runs entirely in the browser; nothing is installed.
- It is optimised for desktop and tablet (landscape).
- It is a **didactic simplification**, not legal advice. For formal institutional
  use, pair it with the actual regulation and, where needed, a legal review.
